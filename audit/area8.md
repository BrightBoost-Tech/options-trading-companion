# AREA 8 — ENTRY-FILL EFFICACY (the accept-side execution-quality lens)

**Adopted:** 2026-07-01 (v5 run 7). **Replaces:** Negative-Decision Efficacy (adopted 2026-06-10; named-for-replacement 2026-06-30; retired this run — see the retirement note at the bottom).

**Rationale for adoption:** The predecessor lens measured whether the pipeline's REJECT decisions were correct. It structurally could not see the quality of its ACCEPTS — and the first live-autopilot trade (SOFI 06-30) failed in exactly that blind spot: a candidate that passed every gate on EV +$30.63, broker-filled at its limit with zero fill slippage, and was still underwater-on-executable from the first second because ~$135 of bid/ask round-trip cost was invisible to the admit decision. To the negative-decision lens this trade was out of scope (it was an accept); to A1 it reads as generic "model optimism"; to A6 it counted as volume. The mechanism — execution cost between the decision price and the executable price — was measurable at stage time and nobody's charter. Second instance of the class: the 06-03 NFLX staged-live order that watchdog-cancelled unfilled after ~5min (an "accept" that produced no position, no outcome, no learning row). The adoption trigger named on 06-30 ("once staged-live data exists") is now met: one complete staged→filled→closed live lifecycle (SOFI), an armed entry round-trip gate (#1101) stamping `blocked_reason='ev_below_roundtrip_cost'`, and close-fill-gap persistence (#1102) writing `close_fill_gap_*` keys into close `order_json` — the lens's evidence base and its forward data feed both exist as of this run.

**Goal:** Measure, in dollars from this system's own data, the gap between the price basis a decision was made on and the price basis reality delivered — at entry (staged limit vs fill vs executable cross), at rest (fill-or-watchdog-cancel rates), and at exit (trigger basis vs achievable vs actual fill) — so that execution-quality regressions become data-detectable and the deferred Phase-3 (exit-trigger basis calibration) reopens on a measured distribution instead of an anecdote.

## Look-at list (each run)

1. **Staged-live inventory (lifetime + day-over-day):** every live-routed staged order: filled / watchdog-cancelled / broker-rejected, time-to-fill, fill price vs staged limit. (Baseline 2026-07-01: N=2 lifetime — NFLX 06-03 watchdog-cancel ~5min; SOFI 06-30 filled at the 1.44 net limit within ~10s of staging. Fill behavior is instant-or-never-shaped; N far too small for rates.)
2. **Entry round-trip gate telemetry (#1101):** count of `ev_below_roundtrip_cost` stage rejections; for each, gross_ev vs round_trip cost from the blocked_detail numbers; classify spread-eaten (correct rejection) vs real-edge-lost (over-rejection candidate — flag for operator, never auto-loosen). (Baseline: gate armed 06-30 22:35Z, ZERO evaluations to date — nothing has reached the stage seam since.)
3. **Close-fill gap distribution (#1102):** `close_fill_gap_fraction` on every close order's order_json; live closes only carry information (shadow/internal fills are AT cross by design — fill-only rows, gap NULL/NA). Track count toward the Phase-3 reopen gate (≥10–15 live close fills). (Baseline: 1 informative datapoint ever, the SOFI 06-30 live close, gap≈0.23, hand-derived pre-deploy and pinned as the module fixture; first runtime event 07-01 was a shadow close → fill-only row persisted correctly, cross/mid NULL by design. Accrual rate ≈ live-close rate ≈ ~1/week → reopen gate is months out at current volume; say so each run.)
4. **Entry-vs-exit basis unification:** entry gate reads `executable_roundtrip_cost` (exit_mark_corroboration.py:344); exit triggers read `achievable_close` via `_executable_for`. Any code change that moves one seam off the shared per-leg executable basis re-creates the entry-admits-what-exit-kills bug (the 06-30 UNIFICATION TRAP note). Verify both seams still read the same primitive after any touch to exit_mark_corroboration.py.
5. **Watchdog interaction:** any staged live order cancelled by the idle watchdog (~90s/poll, DAY limits): count, and whether the cancel was priced (quote moved away) or liquidity (never touched). `cancelled_reason` on paper_orders is the field of record.
6. **Shadow-vs-live fill fidelity:** shadow entries fill at limit+slippage model (SOFI shadow: requested 1.44 → filled 1.4544, +1%); shadow closes fill AT full-cross (#1017). Where a live twin exists, report the live-vs-shadow fill delta so cohort-comparison consumers (policy_lab) know the modeled-fill bias magnitude for that structure class (SOFI pair: live realized −40 vs shadow −1,044.48 on the same thesis — sizing 5 vs 17 contracts plus overnight hold plus open-rotation cross).

## Constraints

- READ-ONLY, same as the whole audit: SELECT-only SQL, broker read-tools only; nothing persisted by the audit itself.
- Executable-side discipline (#1017 lesson): a fill quality judged against mid is an upper bound, not an estimate; judge against the executable side.
- Fill-realism: a staged limit that filled once is not a fill-rate; instant-or-never shape means N accrues slowly — state N every run, never extrapolate from N<10.
- Recommendations must be ADDITIVE observability or tightening only.

## Disqualifiers (what is NOT a finding under this lens)

- Any proposal to loosen the round-trip gate, widen limits, extend watchdog timeouts, or delay exit evaluation to "improve fills" — timing/price loosenings are loss-control loosenings. The lens may only flag for operator investigation.
- A shadow close's gap_fraction (structurally 0/NA — fill IS the cross; zero information content).
- Re-finding the GATED Phase-3 item (exit-trigger full-cross over-pessimism, deferred 2026-06-30 with a designed reopen gate) — this lens's job is to ACCRUE its reopen data, not to re-report the class. Also excluded: the ledgered one-shot/day executor cadence (P1, anti-list), raw-mode EV optimism (#1051 epoch, by design), and the #1017 shadow-mark bias (doctrine).
- N=1 fill anecdotes presented as rates; anything requiring new write permissions, new entitlements, or contract modification.

**HARD BOUNDARY (restating the contract):** Area 8 may never propose loosening a risk control, expanding this loop's write permissions, or modifying the v5 contract sections. This spec content is the only editable surface.
